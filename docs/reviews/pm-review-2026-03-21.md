# Product Manager Review — 2026-03-21

**Reviewer perspective:** PM evaluating Event Radar for product-market fit, monetization readiness, and competitive positioning.

---

## Value Proposition Clarity — 6/10

**Can a new user understand what this does in 10 seconds?**

The landing page says: *"Track market-moving events before they hit the headlines."* That's clear. But the subtitle *"Let's set up your feed in 30 seconds"* immediately pulls users into setup before they understand the VALUE.

**What's missing:** There's no demonstration of what the product actually looks like when it's working. No sample alert, no "here's what you'll get" preview. The user is asked to commit (pick tickers) before they've seen any payoff. For a product that promises to be faster than headlines, you need to SHOW that speed — maybe a live counter of events detected today, or a sample alert that landed 45 minutes before Bloomberg.

**The deeper problem:** The value prop is "event detection." But the product actually delivers "AI-powered event analysis with directional thesis, confidence scoring, and historical pattern matching." That's 10x more valuable than "event detection" and the landing page doesn't communicate any of it.

---

## Information Architecture — 7/10

The five-tab navigation (Feed / Watchlist / Scorecard / History / Settings) is logical:

- **Feed** is the right default. It's where value happens.
- **Watchlist** as tab 2 makes sense — it's the user's configuration of what they care about.
- **Scorecard** is the trust layer. Smart placement — not hidden, not in your face.
- **History** is the archive. Fine as tab 4.
- **Settings** is Settings.

**Issues:**
- The Feed has two modes ("My Watchlist" / "All Events") via a dropdown. This should probably be tabs or a toggle within the feed, not a dropdown. Dropdowns hide options.
- There's no "Dashboard" or "Overview" — the feed IS the overview. For a financial tool, traders expect a dashboard with at-a-glance status: markets up/down, your alerts today, biggest movers on your list. The feed is a chronological list, not a dashboard.
- The Scorecard page is ambitious and important, but it's also overwhelming. Consider splitting it: a simple trust summary on the main scorecard tab, with "Advanced" for the full bucket breakdowns.
- No search page in the main nav. You can search tickers from the watchlist, but there's no global search for events. If a trader hears about "the Iran sanctions thing," they need to find it fast.

## Feature Completeness — 7/10

**What you have that competitors don't:**
- AI-powered bull/bear thesis on every alert. This is genuinely differentiated. No one else auto-generates directional analysis on breaking news.
- Confidence scoring with historical pattern matching (Scorecard). This is a trust innovation — showing users your track record is bold and honest.
- Alert provenance ("Source Journey") showing the pipeline: Rule Filter → AI Judge → Enriched → Delivered. This transparency is rare.
- Multi-source aggregation (CNBC, Yahoo Finance, SEC, FDA, StockTwits, Federal Register, etc.)

**What competitors have that you don't:**
- **Price data.** There are zero stock prices anywhere in the app. A trading tool with no prices is like a weather app with no temperature. You show "BEARISH High conf" but never show the actual stock price or how it moved.
- **Charts.** No price charts, no volume charts, no "what happened to the stock after this event" visualization.
- **Real-time quotes.** The "Live" indicator suggests real-time, but there's no live price ticker.
- **Social proof / crowd signal.** StockTwits sentiment, Twitter volume, Reddit mentions — you ingest StockTwits events but don't show aggregate sentiment.
- **Alerts / notifications that actually work.** Push alerts show "PERMISSION: DENIED" — the core delivery mechanism for a real-time tool is broken in the browser.
- **Portfolio integration.** No way to connect a brokerage account or track positions.
- **Earnings calendar.** Traders live by the earnings calendar. It's missing.

## Onboarding Effectiveness — 8/10

The onboarding is actually well done:

1. Welcome → Get Started (clear CTA)
2. Pick tickers (popular + sector packs + trending + custom input, 3-ticker minimum with live counter)
3. Enable notifications (good explanation of severity tiers)
4. You're all set! (with Scorecard explanation)

**Time-to-value:** About 30 seconds as promised. After onboarding, you land on a feed with events filtered to your watchlist. That's quick.

**What could improve:**
- Show a SAMPLE alert during onboarding so users know what they're signing up for before they commit tickers.
- The notification step is wasted if push is denied (which it was). Offer alternative channels: email, Discord webhook, Telegram. Don't make the user feel like they failed.
- After onboarding, the feed shows events from days ago ("4d ago"). This undermines the "real-time" promise. If there are no fresh events, say so honestly: "No events for your tickers in the last 24h. Here's what happened recently."

## Retention Hooks — 4/10

**What would bring a user back daily?**

Right now? Not much. The problems:

1. **No notification delivery.** The #1 retention hook for a real-time event tool is push notifications. Without working push, users have to actively open the app to check. They won't.
2. **No daily digest.** No morning summary email: "3 events overnight for your watchlist. AAPL had a critical SEC filing."
3. **No streaks or engagement mechanics.** I'm not saying gamify trading (please don't), but a "morning briefing" or "pre-market radar" would give users a reason to open the app at the same time every day.
4. **The feed is mostly noise.** With HIGH and CRITICAL filters on, I see 2-3 events across several days. That's not enough signal to justify daily visits. With filters off, I'd be drowning in Federal Register notices about deep seabed mining. Neither extreme works.
5. **No "what happened next."** After you see a BEARISH alert, you never learn if the prediction was right. The Scorecard shows aggregate stats, but there's no per-alert follow-up: "We said BEARISH on AAL 4 days ago. AAL is down 3.2% since."

**The sticky feature you're missing:** A "this week's P&L impact" calculation. If a trader had acted on your BEARISH signals, how much would they have made/lost? Make the value tangible.

---

## Monetization Readiness — 4/10

**Would someone pay $29/month?**

**No. Not yet.** Here's why:

1. **No price data.** You can't charge traders $29/month for a tool that doesn't show stock prices. They'd need to have TradingView or another platform open alongside Event Radar. That's a hard sell — Event Radar becomes a notification sidebar, not a primary tool.

2. **The hit rate is 24.4%.** The Scorecard proudly displays this. A 24.4% directional hit rate means the AI is wrong 75% of the time. That's worse than a coin flip. Nobody pays $29/month for a 24.4% accuracy rate. This number needs to either improve dramatically or be reframed (e.g., "we flag 100% of critical events — directional analysis is supplementary context, not trading advice").

3. **Free alternatives are good enough.** TradingView alerts + Twitter/X + a free news aggregator cover 80% of what Event Radar does. The AI analysis is the differentiator, but at 24.4% accuracy, it's not differentiated enough.

4. **No premium tier differentiation.** There's no concept of free vs paid. What would paid unlock? Faster alerts? More sources? Custom rules? There's no paywall strategy.

**What would make $29/month justifiable:**
- Real-time price integration with post-alert tracking
- >60% directional hit rate OR framing as "never miss a critical event" (coverage metric) rather than "we predict direction"
- Working push notifications with custom alert rules
- API access for programmatic traders
- Earnings calendar + conference call analysis

## Top 5 Product Improvements (Ranked by Impact)

### 1. Add live price data to event cards and detail pages
**Impact:** Transforms from "news aggregator with AI" to "trading intelligence platform." Every event card should show current price, day change %, and a sparkline. The event detail should show what the price did AFTER the event.
**Effort:** Medium — requires market data API integration (Polygon, Alpha Vantage, or similar).

### 2. Fix or reframe the 24.4% directional hit rate
**Impact:** The Scorecard is currently an anti-selling feature. It tells users the AI is wrong 3 out of 4 times. Either improve the model or change the metric to something meaningful like "event detection rate" or "coverage: we caught X% of market-moving events."
**Effort:** High (model improvement) or Low (reframing).

### 3. Build working notification delivery (email + mobile push + Discord/Telegram)
**Impact:** Without notifications, this is a tool you have to actively check. With notifications, it's a tool that comes to YOU. The difference between a daily-active product and an abandoned tab.
**Effort:** Medium — backend delivery exists (Discord), frontend push is partially built.

### 4. Add a "What Happened Next" follow-up to every alert
**Impact:** Closes the loop. Users see an alert, wonder what happened, and come back to check. This is your retention hook AND your trust builder — "we said BEARISH, and AAL dropped 4.2% in 5 days."
**Effort:** Medium — requires price tracking over T+5 and T+20 windows (infrastructure exists in Scorecard).

### 5. Create a morning briefing / daily digest
**Impact:** Gives users a reason to open the app every morning. "3 events overnight. Here's your pre-market radar." This is the daily habit that drives retention.
**Effort:** Low — content already exists, just needs packaging and delivery.
