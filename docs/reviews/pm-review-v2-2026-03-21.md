# Product Manager Review v2 — 2026-03-21

**Reviewer perspective:** PM evaluating Event Radar for product-market fit, monetization readiness, and competitive positioning.
**Compared to:** pm-review-2026-03-21.md (v1)
**Sprints completed since v1:** S0 (Bug Fixes) → S5 (Smart Feed + Global Search)

---

## Value Proposition Clarity — 7/10 (was 6/10, +1)

**What improved:**
- The Scorecard reframe (S2) is the single biggest messaging win. The hero now shows "24,039 Events Detected" instead of leading with the 24.4% hit rate. This changes the narrative from "we're wrong 75% of the time" to "we're scanning 15 sources 24/7." The hit rate is still there, but buried in Advanced Analytics behind a collapsible section with a disclaimer. Smart move.
- "What is Smart Feed?" tooltip explains the algorithmic curation. Small touch, but it answers the obvious question before the user asks it.

**What's still missing:**
- The onboarding STILL doesn't demonstrate value before asking for commitment. Users pick tickers before seeing a single alert example. Show a demo alert during onboarding — "Here's what you'll get: [sample BEARISH alert on NVDA with bull/bear thesis]."
- The "Viewing delayed public feed / Sign in for live →" banner appears intermittently but there's no clear auth system visible. This creates confusion: is this product free? Freemium? What does "sign in" unlock?
- The landing page value prop hasn't changed: "Track market-moving events before they hit the headlines." This undersells the product. The real value prop is: "AI-powered event analysis with directional thesis, confidence scoring, and 15-source aggregation." The headline promises speed; the product delivers analysis. Misaligned.

## Retention Hooks — 6/10 (was 4/10, +2)

**What improved:**
1. **Daily Briefing** — This is the retention hook the product needed. "📰 Daily Briefing — Saturday, March 21" with event count and top event gives users a reason to open the app. Dismiss is persistent (won't nag you twice on the same day). This is a real daily touchpoint.
2. **Smart Feed** — Curated content > firehose. Users get a feed that's relevant to their watchlist and filtered by quality. This makes the daily visit more rewarding than scrolling through noise.
3. **Event Detail tabs working** — Users can now explore events in depth (Summary → Evidence → Trust) without broken UI. This increases time-on-page and comprehension.

**What's still weak:**
1. **No notification delivery.** Push notifications still don't work (permission denied, no fallback channels). The Daily Briefing only works if you actively open the app. There's no pull mechanism.
2. **No "What happened next."** After seeing a BEARISH alert on AAL 4 days ago, I still can't see what happened to AAL's price. The loop is never closed. This is still the biggest retention gap.
3. **Feed content staleness.** "My Watchlist" mode shows events from 4-5 days ago as the most recent. That's not enough signal to justify daily visits. If there are no fresh events, say "Quiet day for your tickers" instead of showing stale content.

## Monetization Readiness — 4.5/10 (was 4/10, +0.5)

**Would someone pay $29/month?**

**Still no.** The Scorecard reframe helps (you're no longer actively telling users you're wrong 75% of the time), but the fundamental barriers remain:

1. **No price data.** A trading tool without prices requires a second screen. At $29/month, users expect an integrated experience, not a sidebar to TradingView.
2. **The hit rate is still 24.4%.** It's hidden better, but it's still there. Anyone who clicks "Advanced Analytics" will find it. The model hasn't improved; only the presentation has.
3. **Free alternatives still win on core features.** TradingView ($15/month) + Twitter (free) covers 80% of the use case. Event Radar's AI analysis is the differentiator, but without verifiable outcomes, users can't confirm the AI adds value.

**What marginally improved:**
- The Smart Feed demonstrates curation intelligence — it's not just a dumb news aggregator.
- The Daily Briefing adds perceived value — it feels like a product that's working for you.
- The Event Detail tabs being functional makes the AI analysis more accessible and impressive.

**What would move the needle to $29/month:**
- Live price integration (even 15-min delayed)
- Per-event outcome tracking: "We said BEARISH on AAL. AAL is down 4.2% since."
- >50% directional accuracy OR reframing entirely as "event coverage" (drop directional claims)
- Working notification delivery via at least 2 channels
- API access for programmatic traders

## Top 5 Remaining Product Gaps (Ranked by Impact)

### 1. Add live price data to event cards and detail pages
**v1 rank:** #1 | **Status:** UNCHANGED
Still the single biggest gap. Every competitor shows prices. Every trader needs prices. The infrastructure exists (outcome tracking in Scorecard uses price data). Surface it per-event. Show current price, price at event time, and % change since event.

### 2. Add per-event outcome tracking ("What happened next")
**v1 rank:** #4 | **Status:** UNCHANGED (promoted to #2)
The Scorecard already computes T+5 and T+20 moves in aggregate. Surface this per-event on the event detail page. "We said BEARISH. 5 days later: AAL down 3.2%. Verdict: ✅ Correct." This is the feature that builds trust AND retention simultaneously.

### 3. Fix event search navigation (route bug)
**v1 rank:** N/A | **Status:** NEW
The event search was the #1 improvement this sprint. But clicking results causes a 404. This is likely a one-line fix (`/events/` → `/event/`). Prioritize immediately — it's embarrassing to ship a search feature where results are unclickable.

### 4. Build notification delivery alternatives (email, Telegram, Discord)
**v1 rank:** #3 | **Status:** UNCHANGED
Push notifications are blocked in most browser environments. The recovery guidance is excellent, but it doesn't solve the fundamental problem: users need alerts delivered TO them. Discord webhook delivery already exists in the backend. Surface it in settings.

### 5. Create a morning briefing email digest
**v1 rank:** #5 | **Status:** PARTIALLY ADDRESSED
The Daily Briefing card is a step in the right direction. But it only works if you open the app. An email digest at 7:30 AM ("3 events overnight for your watchlist") would drive re-engagement from users who have drifted.
